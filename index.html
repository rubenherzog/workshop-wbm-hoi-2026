About the Workshop

Three days of lectures, tutorials, and collaborative projects in computational neuroscience.

This workshop provides hands-on training in whole-brain modeling and high-order interaction analysis for the study of human brain health using M/EEG and fMRI. The program combines theoretical lectures, reproducible Python tutorials, posters, and collaborative projects, emphasizing the integration of mechanistic simulations with multivariate descriptors of coordinated brain activity beyond pairwise interactions.

  • Whole-brain models: theory-driven simulations of large-scale brain dynamics under structural and dynamical constraints.
  • High-order interaction frameworks: information-theoretic and topological methods characterizing coordinated activity beyond pairs.
  • Reproducible Python workflows connecting mechanistic brain simulations with high-order descriptors.
  • Hands-on tutorials, poster sessions, and small-group collaborative projects.
  • Targeted at students and early-career researchers with experience in scientific Python programming.

Invited Speakers

Four experts in computational neuroscience and brain modeling.

Carlos Coronel

Trinity College Dublin / BrainLat

Research spans computational approaches to aging and dementia, biophysical modeling of neuromodulation, and theoretical neuroscience. Will cover whole-brain modeling approaches including Jansen-Rit and Dynamic Mean Field models.

Marylin Gatica

Northwestern University

Deeply committed to integrating research and mentorship in computational neuroscience. Work focuses on topological high-order analysis and multivariate information-theoretic approaches to brain dynamics.

Joana Cabral

Instituto Superior Técnico, University of Lisbon

Research focuses on Hopf-based whole-brain modeling frameworks and large-scale brain dynamics, with applications in computational neuroimaging and the study of brain function across different cognitive and clinical conditions.

Rubén Herzog

IFISC, Universitat de les Illes Balears

Research spans consciousness, computational neuroscience, and open science using network theory, information theory, and dynamical systems approaches. Will cover high-order interaction-based digital twins and integrated computational neuroimaging pipelines.

How to Apply

Open to students and early-career researchers. No registration fee.

Who can apply

The workshop is primarily aimed at Bachelor, Master, and PhD students, with a limited number of places for postdoctoral researchers. Applicants should have practical experience in scientific Python programming and an interest in neuroimaging, computational neuroscience, complex systems, or mathematical modeling. Prior expertise in whole-brain modeling or high-order interaction methods is not required.

Approximately 10 places are reserved for local UIB participants. Total capacity is 30 selected participants.

Application materials

Applications should include:

  • Curriculum vitae
  • Poster abstract

Selection will be made by the organizers. Participants will be notified by email. There is no registration fee. Participants should bring their own laptop and will receive installation instructions and tutorial materials before the workshop.

Application form coming soon. Contact ruben.herzog@uib.es for enquiries.
