About the Workshop

Three days of lectures, tutorials, and collaborative projects in computational neuroscience.

This workshop provides hands-on training in whole-brain modeling and high-order interaction analysis for the study of the human brain. The program combines theoretical lectures, reproducible Python tutorials, posters, and collaborative projects, emphasizing the integration of mechanistic simulations with multivariate descriptors of collective brain dynamics.

  • Whole-brain models: theory-driven simulations of large-scale brain dynamics under structural and dynamical constraints.
  • High-order interaction frameworks: information-theoretic and topological methods characterizing coordinated activity beyond pairs.
  • Reproducible Python workflows connecting mechanistic brain simulations with high-order descriptors.
  • Hands-on tutorials, poster sessions, and small-group collaborative projects.
  • Targeted at students and early-career researchers with experience in scientific Python programming.
  • Lunch and coffee break will be provided. Accommodation not provided by the organization.

How to Apply

Open to students and early-career researchers. No registration fee.

Who can apply

The workshop is primarily aimed at Master, PhD students, and for postdoctoral researchers. Applicants should have practical experience in scientific Python programming and an interest in neuroimaging, computational neuroscience, complex systems, or mathematical modeling. Prior expertise in whole-brain modeling or high-order interaction methods is not required.

Total capacity is 50 selected participants.

Application materials

Applications should include:

  • Curriculum vitae
  • Motivation letter
  • Poster abstract

Selection will be made by the organizers. Participants will be notified by email. There is no registration fee. Participants should bring their own laptop and will receive installation instructions and tutorial materials before the workshop.

Application form coming soon. Contact ruben.herzog@uib.es for enquiries.
